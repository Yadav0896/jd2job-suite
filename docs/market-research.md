# Jd2Job — Market & Competitor Research

*Compiled: 2026-07. Prices change frequently — re-verify before quoting publicly. Sources linked inline.*

## 1. What Jd2Job is

A unified job-hunt loop in one product:

1. **Auto-Apply (Chrome extension)** — applies on LinkedIn with a **per-JD tailored resume PDF** generated by AI at apply time, plus AI answers to screening questions. Cloud-synced application tracker.
2. **Interview Copilot** — real-time, voice-driven answer suggestions during live interviews (screen + mic capture, Deepgram STT, LLM answers). 10-min trial sessions, 40-min paid sessions.
3. **Mock Interview** — voice-based practice interviewer (conversational Deepgram agent or 5-question step mode) with per-answer and overall evaluations.

Pricing (INR, Razorpay): Base ₹999/mo (5 credits) · Top-up ₹249 (1 credit) · Monthly Unlimited ₹3,999 · Quarterly Unlimited ₹9,999. 1 credit = 1 copilot session or 1 tailored resume.

## 2. Market landscape

Two adjacent categories are converging, and nobody owns the combination:

- **AI auto-apply** (LazyApply, Simplify, JobHire.AI, Sonara, LoopCV, FastApply, Oaki) — high churn, race-to-the-bottom pricing, and a well-documented quality problem: tools blast one static resume everywhere, producing mismatched applications and recruiter blacklisting. LazyApply sits at [2.5/5 user ratings with complaints about mismatched applications and refund friction](https://scale.jobs/blog/is-professional-resume-writing-service-worth-it-real-results).
- **AI interview copilots** (Final Round AI, LockedIn AI, Parakeet AI, LastRound AI, CoPrep) — premium-priced ($25–$150/mo), US-dollar billing, and [Final Round AI has no refund policy](https://lastroundai.com/blog/best-ai-interview-copilot-alternatives-2025-comparison), which generates lasting Reddit/Trustpilot backlash.

The gap: **apply → track → prep → perform** is one user journey sold as 2–3 separate subscriptions. Jd2Job is the only India-priced product covering all of it.

## 4. Jd2Job's defensible wedge

1. **Per-application JD-tailored resume at auto-apply speed.** This is the single biggest quality gap in auto-apply — competitors either blast one resume (LazyApply) or charge $39/wk for tailoring (JobHire.AI). Our extension generates a tailored PDF per job for 1 credit.
2. **The closed loop.** Apply (extension) → auto-tracked (cloud sync dashboard) → prep (mock interview, JD already in context) → perform (live copilot). Each stage feeds the next; competitors sell disconnected point tools.
3. **India-first pricing.** ₹999/mo entry vs $25–$148/mo USD competitors. Razorpay UPI/cards removes the international-card friction that locks out most Indian job seekers. Quarterly unlimited (₹9,999) undercuts Final Round AI's $300/yr while adding auto-apply.
4. **Credit economics.** Credits unify both sides of the product (1 tailored resume = 1 copilot session = 1 credit), which simplifies the mental model and lets users self-select their mix.

## 5. Risks & mitigations

- **LinkedIn ToS.** Automated applying violates LinkedIn's terms; account restriction is the existential risk for every tool in this category. Mitigations: human-paced apply speed, user-initiated sessions (not headless 24/7), honest FAQ copy, and positioning as "assisted applying." Never promise "undetectable."
- **Interview copilot ethics.** Some employers consider live assistance cheating. Mitigation: market the copilot for practice + permitted use cases; keep mock interviews (unambiguous value) prominent.
- **AI cost exposure.** Voice minutes (Deepgram) + LLM calls are metered. Mitigations already in code: 10/40-minute session caps, credit gating, authenticated WS proxies, per-user rate limits.
- **Single-platform dependency.** Extension is LinkedIn-only today. Roadmap candidates: Naukri, Indeed, Wellfound — Naukri matters most for the India wedge.

## 6. Recommendations (priority order)

1. Ship the tailored-resume quality loop first — it's the differentiator; A/B the ATS score display in the popup as the trust signal.
2. Add Naukri support to the extension (India wedge).
3. Free tier: keep 1–2 free credits so users experience a tailored resume before paying (LastRound's free tier is their top acquisition channel).
4. Publish a real refund policy — competitors' no-refund stance is a visible weakness to exploit.
5. Content/SEO: "LazyApply alternatives", "Final Round AI pricing India", "auto apply LinkedIn India" — comparison-page traffic is cheap and high-intent.
